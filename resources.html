Resources

Click on this image for Online Therapy:

  • One of the most important things to consider when choosing a therapist is your connection with this person. The right therapist will be a caring and supportive partner in your depression treatment and recovery.
  • Word of mouth is one of the best ways to find a good therapist. Your friends and family may know someone, or your doctor may be able to refer you to someone.
  • National mental health organizations can also help you find a therapist.
  • If money is an issue, you can go to local senior centers, religious organizations, and community mental health clinics. Some of these places often offer therapy for a low payment.

  • National Suicide Prevention Lifeline: 1-800-273-8255
  • National Hopeline Network : 1-800-442-4673: This hotline is for people who are depressed and are thinking about suicide.
  • Veterans Crisis Line: 1-800-273-8255 This hotline is for vets who struggle with depression and other mental health issues.
  • The Trevor Project: (866) 488-7386: 24-hour depression hotline for suicidal LGBTQ youth.
